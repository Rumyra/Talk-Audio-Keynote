Wax Cylinders

In 1877 Thomas Edison wrapped tin foil around a cylinder. The phonograph was born. The first device to reproduce recorded sound.

It was improved by ALexander Graham Bell's Volta Labratory and wax cylinders became the distributed audio format.

See that's a great quiz question everyone in this room can answer - what's the connection between the telephone and the light bulb.

Playback piano roll

Phonautograph

Nearly 150 years after they had been recorded, promising specimens of Scott de Martinville's phonautograms, ... were located by American audio historians. ... In 2008, the team played back the recordings as sound for the first time. Modern computer-based image processing methods were used to accomplish the playback. ... generally available image-editing and image-to-sound conversion software, requiring only a high-quality scan of the phonautogram and an ordinary personal computer, were found to be sufficient for this application. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonautograph
